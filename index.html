How we make hydrogen

It all starts with biogas produced by agricultural waste, water treatment plants, and landfills. Biogas is mostly methane and methane contains hydrogen.

  • Biogas

    From agricultural waste

    Pigs do it, cows do it. We all do it. All that solid waste and garbage we send to landfills creates methane. Biogas suppliers simply capture the methane and prevent it from entering the atmosphere, where it would contribute to the greenhouse effect.

  • Pipeline

    Biogas delivered to pipeline

    Farmers and landfill operators clean up the captured biogas and then deliver it to the same common carrier pipeline system that supplies homes with natural gas for heating and cooking. At this point, it's truly natural gas.

  • Hydrogen

    Methane is converted to hydrogen

    Each HydrogeNXT station has two steam methane reformers which use pressure and steam to convert the methane in natural gas into hydrogen.
